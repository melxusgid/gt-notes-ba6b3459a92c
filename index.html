Round 1 of your feedback Latest

Pushed this week

All the things you flagged, fixed. Plus a few extras I think you'll like.

Fixes

  • Tapping to check things off works instantly now. The tap was always saving, but the screen wasn't refreshing in time. That's sorted.
  • Adding routines and treatments shows up right away instead of feeling laggy.
  • The calendar uses a different color for each treatment so you can tell them apart at a glance. The legend now lists each one by name.
  • Recent logs in the Symptom Log are tappable. Tap any past day to review or edit what you wrote.

New

  • Long-press the gear icon (top right of Today) to get a quick little menu: light/dark toggle, help page, and a way to walk through the tutorial again.
  • A Help page with a quick tour of every tab.
  • One-tap "restart the tutorial" in case you ever want to see the onboarding again.

Little tweaks

  • The skin care ring (the third one) now tells you what fills it — logging how your skin feels + changing your pillowcase.

To get this update

Open the TestFlight app on your phone. There'll be an Update button next to GlowTracker. Tap it and you're good.

The very first build

Your first invite

This is what you started with.

What's in there

  • Today — your daily morning and evening checklists, with little built-in timers between steps.
  • Treatments — add your own actives (retinoids, acids, vitamin C, whatever), pick the days you use them, optionally with a gradual ramp-up.
  • Closing rings like Apple Fitness — morning, evening, and a third for everyday care. Try to close all three.
  • Calendar showing which nights are treatment nights at a glance.
  • Progress with streaks, a 5-week heatmap, milestones, and a skin-condition trend graph.
  • Journal for weekly progress photos — first vs. latest comparison built in.
  • Symptom Log for redness, peeling, dryness, irritation, and breakouts.
  • Social — profiles, messaging, and borrowing routine steps from friends.
  • Six pastel themes, light and dark mode, and a cozy dark mode that isn't harsh.
  • Onboarding with a hands-on tutorial so you know how it all works.
